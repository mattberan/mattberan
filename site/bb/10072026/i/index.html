Information is power; and I've been trying to track my AI impact in data.

It's not easy, it's very manual and I doubt it's accurate. But you're got to start somewhere right?

So I've been using EcoLogits calculator and a few others just to see if they are even in the same ballpark.

They aren't.

So I'm guessing that a lot of this is conjecture... and down the rabbit hole I go.

Here are some tips for getting started:
1 - agentic sessions burn far more compute than a single chat message - this one is obvious, but your measures need to account for this
2 - Use 0.3 Wh as your baseline for a typical text prompt - a few providers have gone on the record to say that is a pretty good estimate.
3 - Google's figure is about 0.26 mL per prompt for water consumption (for cooling I think)

And that's just for public models...

What continues to pique my curiosity is how teams are going to be measuring this and estimating this. Sure you can go to tokens, but that's only a proxy for how much impact - it's more effective for calculating SPEND - not IMPACT.

Will this be the job of an asset manager? A sysadmin? Who owns this kind of thing - and is that the right place?
