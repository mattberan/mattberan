The Take

Being that I'm not impressed with bi-lateral capitalism, I've been looking into alternative system thinking; fascinating ideas!

I've never been a huge fan of capitalism. Don't get me wrong, I like the idea of entrepreneurship, and believe we all have the right to earn a living and improve our lives. But seriously... something's not working here if we've got yet another extreme imbalance of wealth.

Degrowth is probably one of my favorites, along with ecocentrism; and if you know me you're not surprised.

However, I had never heard the term "Georgism" - where land and resource value belongs to us all and is thusly taxed. Guess what it helps with: wealth disparity.

No wonder I've never heard of it!

The logic is that a huge share of wealth gains is unearned land/location rent, not productive effort.

What a powerful concept, I guess some other benefits include reduced speculation and more financial system stability.

There are issues of course, it's hard to accurately asses value per se... but I think we could incorporate at least SOME of this into our taxation system? At least some kind of balance.

The last bit I'll put in here is something generally called "Anti-monopoly" or "Commons-based thinking" and those words describe generally, the legislation required to break up concentrated market power, restore competition and treat resources as commons - not private.

Mmmmm... gotta get me some of that!

I'll be looking for language and leaders that align with these concepts.
