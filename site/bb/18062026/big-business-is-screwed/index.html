The Take

Big business is screwed - you no longer need an army of people to operate high value ventures; it turns out AI WILL take your job.

Hot on the heels of another layoff ($erviceNow calls them restructuring so they don't have to use that term, but imagine you are given a job you don't want...) I'm reminded of how imbalanced the world is and how much power has shifted.

It's super-important to always operate like your job is not a guarantee. Terrible advice when someone has lost their job, but great advice years before. This is one of the reasons I garden, jobs aren't guaranteed, but hunger is!

The problem with AI, is that companies of a smaller size can now compete at higher levels. This power shift means that larger, publicly traded companies are losing talent because that's what you do when you are shifting around skills. You can't just keep people on the balance sheet to re-train them.

This goes for companies run by VC or management firm majorities. They put efficiency first, so if you can lean up; you do. Then you trim the fat to pick up some more calories to run the engine again.

It's insane and abusive and I call that out in my recent LinkedIn post.

So what does this mean MACRO? Does this mean we will have more companies? My guess: yes - more diversity, more options, more niche, more custom... more human.

My guess is that as we see this downsizing happening - as smaller companies are more proficient... we will see more entrepreneurs, more startups, more contract-to-hire and this innovation is going to fundamentally change our economy, retirement systems and a whole lot more.

It's a bold take, but it makes sense right? I would love to hear your thoughts - so EMAIL ME BACK ALREADY! ;)
