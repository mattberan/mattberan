The Take

When a system is constrained by markets or supply chain, abuse opportunities arise.

Stay with me here, because I bought a CAR off of Facebook Marketplace.

And while handing a stranger $10k in cash is the weirdest feeling ever, I was just happy to get a well performing car while their prices skyrocket.

If you haven't shopped for a used car recently: don't - you'll hurt.

And as such, the scams on used car platforms are EVERYWHERE.

So here's the story:
1 - One of our friends new we were searching for a car.
2 - They saw their friend posted one nearby on Facebook.
3 - They send me the link and I reach out - and it's a great price so i REALLY want it.
4 - They ask for a deposit to hold it for me because so many people want it
5 - I think they are a friend of a friend and I send it
6 - I realize my mistake and find out the friend's friend's facebook account was hacked

I hang my head in shame as I realize I went straight to #5 too quickly.

I get the feeling that this is going to get much, much worse as resources and wealth disparity increases.

And I saw it in my bank's newsletter - a reminder to SLOW DOWN, don't act with emotion and that if someone is treating a normal interaction as URGENT - that is a yellow (or even red) flag.

So computer nerds - this is the work. Spread the word, prepare your loved ones and YOURSELF.

Everything you need to know. Nothing you don't.
